# LinkedIn post template — Tatiana voice

Use for any LinkedIn post — observation, course, project, reflection. The shape is constant; the components swap. Drafts to `data/drafts/`. Never auto-publishes.

---

## Frontmatter check (run before drafting)

**Which identity is leading?**

| Identity | When it leads |
|---|---|
| Work-Tatiana | Projects she shipped at Shopify, team shoutouts, structural observations from the day job |
| Tatiana the Visionary | Creative work, the newsletter, broader life observations |
| Virtual Tatiana | Agentic commerce, AI prototypes, the visionary future-pull |
| Human-Tatiana | Genuinely personal — tax day, deferred decisions, family, partnership — structural rigor on her own life |

Valid hybrid patterns: work-led/virtual-flavored, virtual-led/human-flavored, visionary-led/work-anchored, human-led/work-anchored.

Anti-pattern: work-led/human-flavored. Reads as oversharing in a professional setting unless the topic is genuinely shared.

If ambiguous, ask Tatiana before drafting. The identity decision shapes voice register; getting it wrong wastes the draft.

## Component shape (the constant)

### 1. Observational opening (1-2 sentences)

`<concrete observation — what's been on the network's mind today, or what Tatiana noticed>`

Examples that work:
- "Today I've seen a lot of posts about finances, kids, the conversations people seem to have only when the deadline forces them."
- "A while back I took a 27-minute course."

Anti-pattern: "The structural pattern I want to discuss" (abstract, doesn't ground)

### 2. Bridge (1 fragment or sentence)

`<2-3 word punchy line — "Cascades happen." / "Same shape." / "Same pattern.">`

The bridge announces the structural reading is coming. Don't skip it.

### 3. Tool / course / concept + source chain (1-3 sentences)

`<the framework or course referenced>`

If the source came from someone else: name them with @-mention.
`<@<peer name> shared this on his/her feed.>`

### 3b. Honest deferral admission (optional, 1-2 sentences)

If Tatiana saw the resource months ago and didn't engage with it until now:

`<I saw it. I didn't click. Today I finally did.>`

The deferral admission IS the post's most relatable move. Use it when it's true.

### 4. The thesis underlined (1 sentence, **bold**)

`<the single quotable line that's the share-able takeaway>`

**Only one bold line per post.** Multiple bolds dilute the share-able sentence.

Lines that have landed:
- **"The questions you don't ask are the deferred decisions in your life."**
- **"The cascade you didn't choose is shaped by the questions you didn't ask."**

### 5. Work-to-life bridge (4-6 sentences)

`<3-4 parallel examples of the same structural pattern operating in two different domains>`

This is the post's most important move. Examples from one domain (work or life), then the same shape in the other domain.

Ironic-recognition move: when the post is about NOT applying the structural lens in her personal life, the bridge owns the irony. "At work, I notice when systems are degrading. At home, I let one degrade for two years." Lands hard.

### 6. The "real question" line (1-2 sentences)

`<a simple statement about how the move actually works in practice>`

The sentence that gives permission to do the thing.

### 7. Productive-question close (1 sentence)

`<an open-ended question that meta-honors the post's lesson>`

Real, not rhetorical. Answerable, not vague.

Closing questions that work:
- "What's the question you've been avoiding asking?"
- "What's the structural problem you've been treating as tactical?"
- "What's the system you're compensating for that should be designed instead?"

Forbidden closers: "Thoughts?" / "Agree?" / "Anyone else feel this way?" / "Drop a comment if..."

### 8. Source-chain courtesy close (optional)

`<(Thanks @<peer> for the nudge — sometimes the click that matters most is the one we keep deferring.)>`

A parenthetical thanks, with a meta-line tying back to the thesis. Optional, lands when it's earned.

## Word count — hard ceiling

200-300 words. 305 acceptable if the closing line is doing real work. Anything over 350 gets scroll-skipped.

---

## Day-job phrasing — the hard rule

What Tatiana gets paid for: **Enterprise Technical Support Expert at Shopify Enterprise Support.** Solves high-stakes problems for Shopify's largest merchants every day.

The lens she brings: cascade pattern thinking, structural reading, system-design instincts.

These are NOT the same thing. Conflating them over-claims.

| ❌ Wrong | ✓ Right |
|---|---|
| "I'm someone who builds systems for a living" | "For a living, I'm an Enterprise Technical Support Expert at Shopify Enterprise Support" |
| "As a TPM at Shopify..." | "For a living, I'm an Enterprise Technical Support Expert at Shopify Enterprise Support" |
| "I write product proposals all year" | "The pattern I keep noticing in this work..." |

## Format rules

- **Single line between paragraphs.** LinkedIn renders these as paragraph breaks; double-line breaks waste vertical space.
- **Don't bury the link.** Inline with a soft phrase: "Worth the 27 minutes:"
- **Italicize quoted thoughts.** *"Why does this keep breaking?"* — visual cue.
- **No hashtags by default.** If genuinely needed (rare): `#cascade` or `#productivequestions`. Never more than 2.
- **No emoji** — or one strategic emoji max (a single closing 💜 on a celebration moment).
