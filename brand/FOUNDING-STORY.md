## The long version (About page)

> ### Our Story
>
> Alleyoop Arts was founded by Damion Rashford, a builder whose path runs from the court, through code, to culture.
>
> Damion grew up in Pickering, Ontario. At seventeen he moved alone to Phoenix, Arizona to chase a Division I lane — and earned it, captaining Westwind Prep to All-Arizona honors before joining Loyola University Maryland. What followed wasn't a highlight reel. It was a maze: a redshirt year, a bench season, a bet-on-yourself detour through a Nebraska junior college where he averaged 15 a game, a final stop in Detroit — and then the hardest play in basketball: knowing when to pass.
>
> He passed the ball to his next self. Damion built a career in technology, where he works with Shopify's developer ecosystem and creates open-source AI tools used by builders around the world. The discipline transferred. The love of the game never left.
>
> Alleyoop Arts is where it landed. The brand's north star — *basketball as a labyrinth: finding a lane, reading the floor, improvising under pressure* — isn't a metaphor someone wrote in a brainstorm. It's the route Damion actually ran, across five schools, two countries, and a decade of corridors that didn't go straight. The maze in every poster is autobiography.
>
> And the name is the thesis. An alley-oop is the one play you cannot run alone. Someone reads the floor, someone trusts the lob, someone finishes. We make art, stories, and experiences that throw that pass — to the athletes, artists, and voices who make this game beautiful.
>
> The pass is the play.

## Voice rules for telling this story

- **Third person** for Damion on web/deck/press. "We" for the organization. "I" only in a signed founder's letter.
- **The juco chapter stays in.** 15.1 PPG at Western Nebraska is the most founder-shaped number in the arc — the bet on yourself when the straight path closed. Cutting it makes the story corporate.
- **The ending is a handover, not a failure.** He didn't "quit basketball" — he passed the ball to his next self. The walking-away beat is what makes the founding credible: he came back to the game on his own terms, as an author.
- **No job titles.** "Works with Shopify's developer ecosystem while building open-source AI tools." Builder framing, not employee framing.
- **No fabricated stakes.** No "almost went pro," no invented adversity. The real arc — move alone at 17, redshirt, bench, juco, walk away, build — is strong enough without decoration.
