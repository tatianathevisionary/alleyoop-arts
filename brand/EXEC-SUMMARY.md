# Alleyoop — Executive Summary

One page. June 2026. Full plan: `BUSINESS-PLAN.md`.

---

**What we are.** Alleyoop is a Canadian non-profit cultural organization that elevates
basketball as art, culture, and storytelling. We produce a premium digital magazine,
limited-edition art prints, and experiences celebrating Canadian athletes, artists,
and voices.

**Founder.** Alleyoop was founded by Damion Rashford, a former college basketball
player from Pickering, Ontario. His route — Phoenix prep ball, Division I at Loyola
University Maryland, a juco rebuild in Nebraska, the decision to walk away — became
the brand's north star: *basketball as a labyrinth*. He went on to build a career in
technology, where he works with Shopify's developer ecosystem and creates open-source
AI tools. Alleyoop is his return to the game as an author.

**Positioning.** Canada's premium platform for basketball culture. Not another stats
site — the gallery for the game.

**Why now.** Canadian basketball is producing world-class talent and a fast-growing
culture, with no high-end, artistically driven platform dedicated to it. Alleyoop
fills that gap — and starts with a catalog of 156 original artworks and
production-ready art pipelines already built.

**Model.** Sustainable non-profit, four revenue streams:
- Limited art print + merch sales (Shopify)
- Magazine subscriptions + print editions
- Corporate sponsorships (partnership, not ad space)
- Grants + donations

**Brand.** Ink black, court cream, one orange — the ball's. Type-only wordmark, two
rims in the double O. Tagline: **The pass is the play.**

**Year 1.**
- Launch brand, site, Shopify store
- Publish 8–12 digital issues
- Release first limited print collection
- 3,000–5,000 email subscribers; first sponsorship or grant

**Vision.** Treat the game with the artistic depth given to music, film, and visual
arts — Canada first, then the world.
