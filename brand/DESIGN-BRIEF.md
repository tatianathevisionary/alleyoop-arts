# ALLEYOOP ARTS — Design Brief

Self-contained handoff document. Everything a designer (or design AI) needs to produce
the brand identity and site/store design. No external context required.

Version 1 · June 2026

---

## 1. What this brand is

**Alleyoop Arts** is a Canadian basketball art + culture brand and non-profit.
It produces:

1. A **digital magazine** (site-first → annual print edition) celebrating Canadian
   athletes, artists, and voices
2. A **Shopify store** selling limited-edition art prints (a catalog of 156 original
   basketball artworks already exists) and select merch
3. **Events and experiences** — exhibitions, panels, community programs

**Positioning:** the gallery for the game. Premium culture brand, not sports media.
Think Victory Journal / Franchise Mag energy, not a stats site. It must look credible
next to major sponsor logos while staying rooted in streetball warmth.

**Tagline:** *The pass is the play.*

---

## 2. The founder story (the soul of the brand — design should feel like this)

Damion Rashford grew up in Pickering, Ontario. At seventeen he moved alone to
Phoenix, Arizona to chase a Division I basketball lane — and earned it: team captain,
All-Arizona honors, then Loyola University Maryland. What followed wasn't a highlight
reel. It was a maze: a redshirt year, a bench season, a bet-on-himself detour through
a Nebraska junior college (15.1 points per game), a final stop in Detroit — and then
the hardest play in basketball: knowing when to pass. He handed the ball to his next
self, built a career in technology, and returned to the game as an author — founding
Alleyoop Arts.

**Why this matters for design:** the brand's north star is *basketball as a
labyrinth — finding a lane, reading the floor, improvising under pressure.* The maze
motif runs through the entire art catalog. The name is the thesis: an alley-oop is
the one play you cannot run alone — one person throws the lob, another finishes.
Two parts, one play.

**Brand values:** The pass is the play (credit and elevate others) · Find a lane
(celebrate navigation, not just arrival) · Bet on yourself (back the unproven) ·
Know when to hand it over (honor every version of a basketball life) · Build, don't
borrow (author the culture, own the platform).

## 3. Color system (locked — do not propose alternatives)

| Color | Hex | Role |
|-------|-----|------|
| **Ink Black** | `#0E0E0E` | Type, logo, dark surfaces. Soft black, not `#000` |
| **Court Cream** | `#F2E9C7` | Primary background — paper, print, gallery wall |
| **Alleyoop Orange** | `#EE9933` | The ONE accent. It's the ball/rim color in all 156 existing artworks |
| Charcoal Teal | `#223333` | Optional second dark for warm surfaces (packaging interiors, footers) |

**Rules:**
- Cream leads. Default = Court Cream surface + Ink Black type (editorial/print mode)
- Black flips for dark mode, social, merch: Ink Black surface + Court Cream type.
  Orange stays orange in both modes
- Orange is scarce: one accent per composition, never more than twice per screen
- **Never** navy, gold, gradients, or new colors at the brand layer (a navy/gold
  luxury direction was explicitly considered and rejected)
- Photography: black & white by default; full color is reserved for the artwork
  itself. Monochrome wrapper + colorful art = the signature contrast
- The art collections have their own palettes (maze teal, sunset orange, sky blue,
  etc.). The brand wrapper stays neutral so every poster pops. The wrapper is the
  gallery; the art is the show

## 4. Logo (approved direction — execute, don't reinvent)

**Type-only. No crests, no shields, no basketball clip-art, no player silhouettes,
no swooshes.** The letterforms are the logo.

### Approved reference DNA (two real-world references the founder picked)

1. **"OXEYE hair studio"-style lockup** — bold geometric caps wordmark where the O is
   a large clean circle outline, paired with a small handwritten-script secondary line
   and "EST." details
2. **"g&g"-style monogram** — stacked/overlapping lowercase letterforms with
   personality, rendered tone-on-tone (dark on dark)

### Primary: the wordmark

- **ALLEYOOP** in a bold modern grotesque (Neue Haas Grotesk, Söhne, or Satoshi —
  one face only), tracked tight, all caps
- **The signature move: the double-O rims.** The two O's in ALLEY**OO**P drawn as
  clean circle outlines — two basketball rims. One is the pass, one is the finish
- Optional: a single thin lob arc over the OO in Alleyoop Orange — the only color in
  the mark
- Lockup: ALLEYOOP (hero) + "arts" in a small human script or light lowercase
  underneath + `EST. 2026` flanking details on full versions
- "arts" and EST. drop entirely on merch and avatars — ALLEYOOP stands alone

### Secondary: the monogram

Two overlapping lowercase **o**'s, tone-on-tone (cream-on-black or black-on-cream),
soft letterforms with character. For avatars, favicons, print stamps, embroidery,
packaging seals.

---

## 5. Typography

- **Display/headlines:** the same grotesque as the wordmark, bold weight
- **Body:** same family, regular weight, generous leading — editorial readability
- **Accent script:** one handwritten/script face, used ONLY for the "arts" line and
  occasional artist-signature moments. Never for body text
- No more than two typefaces total in the system

---

## 6. Voice (for any copy in mockups)

Confident but welcoming. Street-smart but thoughtful. Proudly Canadian without being
cheesy. Third person for the founder, "we" for the organization.

- ✅ "This is what Canadian basketball looks like right now."
- ✅ "From the block to the gallery."
- ✅ "The pass is the play."
- ❌ "Ball is life", grind/hustle clichés, hype-launch-speak, luxury-brand pretension

---

## 7. Deliverables requested

### Phase 1 — Identity
1. Wordmark explorations (3–5): ALLEYOOP with double-O rims, with/without the orange
   lob arc, caps treatments
2. Full lockup: wordmark + "arts" script + EST. 2026
3. Monogram: overlapping oo, tonal versions both directions
4. Mini brand sheet: logo on cream, on black, clear-space and misuse examples

### Phase 2 — Applications
5. Shopify store homepage concept: cream editorial layout, poster art as the hero
   color moments, black nav/footer
6. Magazine masthead + article page template (B&W photography, cream paper feel)
7. Social templates: avatar (monogram), post frame, story frame — black mode
8. One print mockup: poster in a frame with an Alleyoop Arts gallery label
   (title / edition number / the monogram as a seal)

### Constraints
- 2:3 portrait is the canonical art ratio (the poster catalog is built on it)
- Every layout must survive having a loud, colorful poster dropped into it — that's
  the test of the neutral wrapper
- Sponsor-logo row will appear on site/deck footers: design must hold its own next
  to major brand marks without imitating luxury aesthetics
