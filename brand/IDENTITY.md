## The Story (site/public version — brand-first, light founder mention)

Basketball has always been more than a game. It's the court that holds a
neighborhood together, the route a kid maps for their whole life, the art form
played at full speed. But the way it gets covered — stats, highlights, hot takes —
rarely treats it that way.

Alleyoop Arts exists to fix that. We make limited-edition art, a magazine, and
experiences that give the game what it's always deserved: beauty, depth, and
permanence.

The name is the belief. An alley-oop is the one play you cannot run alone. Someone
reads the floor. Someone trusts the lob. Someone finishes. Everything we make is
that pass — thrown to the athletes the highlight reels missed, the artists the
galleries haven't found yet, and the communities the courts hold together.

Alleyoop Arts was founded by Damion Rashford, a former college basketball player
from Pickering, Ontario who came back to the game through art. The rest of the
story is in the work.

## Mission

**To elevate basketball culture through art, storytelling, and community — starting in Canada.**

We make collectible art, a premium magazine, and experiences worthy of the people who make the game beautiful: the athletes the highlight reels missed, the artists the galleries haven't found yet, the communities the courts hold together.

## Vision

**A world where basketball is treated with the artistic depth given to music, film, and visual arts — and Canada is where that standard gets set.**

The game already is culture, identity, and art. Our job is to build it a permanent home: the gallery for the game.

## Values

Public-facing copy is about the game, not the founder. (Each value traces to a real
chapter of the founding route — receipts live in `FOUNDING-STORY.md` for internal
grounding and press deep-dives, not on the site.)

### 1 · The pass is the play
An alley-oop doesn't exist without trust between two people. We credit everyone,
share the platform, and measure success by who we elevate. The assist counts as much
as the finish.

### 2 · Find a lane
Every route worth taking is a maze. We celebrate navigation, not just arrival — the
transfers, the rehab years, the detours that build the player.

### 3 · Bet on yourself
When the straight path closes, you drop down, do the work, and earn the way back.
We back the unproven: emerging athletes, unsigned artists, voices without a platform
yet.

### 4 · Know when to hand it over
Walking away isn't quitting — it's a pass to your next self. We honor every version
of a basketball life: the player, the coach, the parent, the artist, the fan who
never stopped.

### 5 · Build, don't borrow
We author our culture rather than license someone else's. Original art, original
stories, our own store, our own terms.

## Identity at a Glance

| Element | The call |
|---------|----------|
| **Name** | Alleyoop Arts (parent) · Alleyoop (magazine) · Alleyoop Shop (store) |
| **Tagline** | The pass is the play. |
| **Positioning** | The gallery for the game — culture brand, not sports media |
| **Palette** | Ink Black `#0E0E0E` · Court Cream `#F2E9C7` · Alleyoop Orange `#EE9933` |
| **Logo** | Type-only ALLEYOOP wordmark; the double O drawn as two rims — the pass and the finish |
| **Type** | One bold grotesque + one human script (the "arts" line only) |
| **Imagery** | B&W photography; full color reserved for the art itself |
| **Voice** | Confident but welcoming. Street-smart but thoughtful. Proudly Canadian without being cheesy |
| **Never** | Navy, gold, crests, basketball clip-art, silhouettes, grind clichés |

**Why this palette:** monochrome wrapper, one signature color — the culture-magazine formula. The orange isn't generic basketball orange; it's lifted from our own catalog, the ball-and-rim color across all 156 original artworks. The wrapper is the gallery; the art is the show.

**Why type-only:** the letterforms carry the story. Two O's, two rims — one throws the lob, one finishes. The whole brand in two letters.
