# Alleyoop Arts — Brand Guidelines

The brand wrapper for everything Alleyoop ships: store, magazine, decks, social, events.
Founding narrative lives in `FOUNDING-STORY.md`. Art-collection visual rules live in
`../collections/basketball-posters/ALLEYOOP_ARTS_STYLE_GUIDE.md` — the two systems are
deliberately separate (see "Two-layer system" below).

Last updated: 2026-06-06

---

## Positioning

**Alleyoop Arts is a basketball art and culture brand.**

Canada-first, global DNA. We treat the game with the artistic depth given to music,
film, and visual arts — collectible prints, premium storytelling, and experiences that
celebrate the athletes, artists, and voices who make basketball beautiful.

**Core idea:** *The pass is the play.* An alley-oop is the one play you cannot run
alone — someone reads the floor, someone throws the lob, someone finishes. Everything
the brand does is an assist.

**Architecture:**

| Entity | Role |
|--------|------|
| **Alleyoop Arts** (parent, non-profit) | The cultural organization — mission, programs, sponsorships |
| **Alleyoop** (the magazine/platform) | Site-first digital magazine → annual print edition |
| **Alleyoop Shop** (Shopify) | Limited art prints, magazine, select merch — the commercial arm |

## Values (each one has a receipt)

Every value is tethered to a verified chapter of the founder's arc — see the source
table in `FOUNDING-STORY.md`. Values without receipts are poster words; these are
biography.

### 1. The pass is the play
*From the name itself.* An alley-oop doesn't exist without trust between two people.
We credit contributors, share the platform, and measure success by who we elevate —
athletes, artists, photographers, writers — not by who we outshine.

### 2. Find a lane
*From the maze.* Pickering → Phoenix → Baltimore → Nebraska → Detroit. The route to
anywhere worth going is a labyrinth: finding a lane, reading the floor, improvising
under pressure. We celebrate navigation, not just arrival — the juco seasons, the
redshirt years, the detours that built the player.

### 3. Bet on yourself
*From Western Nebraska, 2014-15: 15.1 PPG on 51.2% FG.* When the straight path closed,
the founder dropped down a level, did the work, and earned the way back. We back the
unproven: emerging athletes, unsigned artists, voices without a platform yet.

### 4. Know when to hand it over
*From January 2016.* The hardest play in basketball is the pass you make when the shot
isn't yours anymore. Walking away from the game wasn't quitting — it was a handover to
the next self. We honor every version of a basketball life: the player, the coach, the
parent, the artist, the fan who never stopped.

### 5. Build, don't borrow
*From the second career.* The founder builds his own tools — open-source AI
infrastructure, this brand itself. We author our culture rather than licensing
someone else's: original art, original stories, our own platform, our own terms.

## Color system

### The decision (2026-06-06)

Black, warm cream, and basketball orange. The classic culture-magazine formula —
monochrome wrapper, one signature color. Chosen over the navy/gold "luxury crest"
direction because (a) it reads *culture*, not liquor sponsorship, and (b) the brand
wrapper must stay neutral so the art collections (each with its own palette) pop
against it instead of clashing.

### Brand palette

| Color | Hex | Role |
|-------|-----|------|
| **Ink Black** | `#0E0E0E` | Type, logo, dark surfaces. Soft black — richer than `#000` on screens, prints true |
| **Court Cream** | `#F2E9C7` | Primary background. Paper, print, gallery wall. Already the highlight color across the poster catalog |
| **Alleyoop Orange** | `#EE9933` | The one accent. Taken from the art itself — it's the ball/rim color in all 156 originals. Links, underlines, the lob-arc in the logo, price tags, CTAs |
| Charcoal Teal | `#223333` | Optional second dark — for surfaces that need warmth (packaging interiors, footers). Also from the style guide |

### Usage rules

- **Cream leads.** Default surface is Court Cream with Ink Black type. This is the
  editorial/print mode — it's what makes the brand read as culture, not tech.
- **Black flips.** Dark mode (site, social, merch) inverts: Ink Black surface, Court
  Cream type. Orange stays orange in both modes.
- **Orange is scarce.** One accent per composition. If orange appears more than twice
  on a screen, it's no longer signal.
- **Never** introduce navy, gold, or gradient treatments at the brand layer.
- **Photography:** black & white by default for editorial/culture content; full color
  reserved for the art itself. This keeps monochrome wrapper + colorful art as the
  signature contrast.

### Two-layer system (brand vs. art)

| Layer | Palette | Governed by |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **Brand wrapper** (logo, site chrome, packaging, decks, social templates) | Black / cream / orange only | This document |
| **The art** (poster collections) | Each collection's own palette (maze teal core, neon night, hyperreal city, …) | `ALLEYOOP_ARTS_STYLE_GUIDE.md` |

The wrapper is the gallery; the art is the show. The gallery never competes with the show.

## Logotype

### Reference direction (approved 2026-06-06)

Two references set the taste level — both type-only, monochrome, one personality
moment:

1. **"g&g" monogram** — stacked overlapping lowercase serif letterforms, tone-on-tone
   (dark on dark). Takeaway: the tonal monogram treatment, letterforms with character.
2. **"OXEYE hair studio"** — bold geometric caps wordmark where the O is a large
   circle outline, paired with a small handwritten script secondary line and "EST."
   detail. Takeaway: the studio lockup formula — loud wordmark, quiet human script.

No crests, no icons, no silhouettes. The letterforms are the logo.

### Primary: the wordmark (OXEYE-style lockup)

**ALLEYOOP** set in a bold modern grotesque (Neue Haas Grotesk, Söhne, or
Satoshi — license one, don't stack three), tracked tight, all caps.

**The signature move: the double-O rims.** The two O's in ALLEY**OO**P drawn as
clean circle outlines — two rims. One is the pass, one is the finish. OXEYE has one
ring; we have two, and ours mean something. Optional: a single thin lob arc over the
OO in Alleyoop Orange — the only color in the mark.

**The lockup:**
- ALLEYOOP — bold caps wordmark, the hero
- "arts" — small secondary line in a human script or light lowercase, under or beside
- `EST. 2026` flanking details on full lockups (packaging, print masthead)
- "arts" and EST. details drop entirely on merch and avatars where ALLEYOOP stands alone

### Secondary: the monogram (g&g-style)

Two overlapping lowercase **o**'s — tone-on-tone (cream-on-black or black-on-cream),
soft letterforms with personality. Used for: avatars, favicons, print stamps,
embroidery, the seal on packaging. This is the g&g move translated: the quiet, tonal,
collectible mark.

### What we don't do

- No crests or shields (luxury cosplay)
- No basketball-texture lettering, no swooshes, no player silhouettes
- No more than one display face; body text is the same family in regular weight

## Voice

**Confident but welcoming. Street-smart but thoughtful. Proudly Canadian without
being cheesy.** Third person for the founder, "we" for the organization, "I" only in
signed founder's letters.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "This is what Canadian basketball looks like right now." | "Ball is life" (unless a contributor says it) |
| "From the block to the gallery." | Generic grind/hustle clichés |
| "The pass is the play." | Hype-salesy launch-speak |
| Name every contributor — photographer, writer, athlete | Uncredited work, stock anything |

**Tagline (primary):** *The pass is the play.*
**Alternates in rotation:** *Elevate. Create. Belong.* · *Where the game meets art.* ·
*Finding a lane.* (lifted straight from the art's north star)

## Sponsor posture

We sell association with *culture*, not ad space. Sponsors get: premium brand
adjacency, an engaged hard-to-reach audience (basketball × art × 18-35), non-profit
partnership credibility, and co-created content (co-branded print editions, art
series, events) — never banner ads. The deck leads with the founder story; the story
is the credibility.
