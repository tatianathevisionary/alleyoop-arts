# ALLEYOOP ARTS — Voice Guide

How the brand sounds, everywhere it speaks. The visual identity makes people look;
the voice makes them stay.

---

## The voice in one line

**A player who became an author.** Someone who's been in the locker room *and* the
gallery — talks like the court, thinks like an editor.

## The four traits

### 1 · Earned, not hyped
We've run the route, so we don't shout. No "🔥 INSANE drop!!", no manufactured
urgency. The confidence is quiet because it's real. We state things; we don't sell
them.

- ✅ "Twelve pieces. Each one numbered, each one signed."
- ❌ "Don't sleep on this exclusive limited heat!!"

### 2 · Warm, not soft
Streetball is generous — you call the next game, you pass to the new kid. The voice
welcomes people in. But warm doesn't mean mushy: we say the hard, true thing about
the game (the bench years, the walk-away) without flinching or therapizing.

- ✅ "Most basketball stories end on the bench. That's where the interesting ones start."
- ❌ "Everyone's journey is valid and beautiful in its own way 💛"

### 3 · Specific, not generic
The detail is the credibility. Name the court, the year, the number, the person.
Generic sports-talk is how you sound like everyone else.

- ✅ "15.1 a game in Scottsbluff, Nebraska, when nobody was watching."
- ❌ "He overcame adversity through hard work and dedication."

### 4 · Plain words, sharp rhythm
Short sentences. Concrete nouns. The poetry comes from rhythm and truth, not
adjectives. If a sentence works without the word, cut the word.

- ✅ "Someone throws the lob. Someone finishes. That's the whole brand."
- ❌ "We passionately leverage the transformative synergy of sport and artistry."

## Tone by surface

Same voice, different volume.

| Surface | Volume | Notes | Example |
|---------|--------|-------|---------|
| **Magazine features** | Full editorial | Long-form earns long sentences. Literary but never academic. | "The court at Bathurst and Dundas has no nets. It has never needed them." |
| **Product / shop copy** | Quiet, factual | The art does the talking. Title, edition, size, story in 2 sentences max. | "Lost Ball Mystery. Edition of 50, signed. The clue is the ball." |
| **Instagram / TikTok captions** | Conversational, lowercase ok | One thought per post. Let the visual lead. | "drop 01. twelve lanes through the maze." |
| **Sponsor / partner decks** | Composed, confident | Full sentences, zero slang, but still us — never corporate beige. | "We don't sell ad space. We co-create culture." |
| **Founder's letters** | First person, intimate | The only "I" surface. Where the maze gets personal. | "I didn't quit basketball. I passed it to the person I was becoming." |
| **Community / events** | Warmest register | Invitational, names people, thanks people. | "Pull up. First run goes to whoever shows up early." |

## The lexicon

### Words we own (use deliberately, not constantly)
- **the lane / finding a lane** — navigation, opportunity
- **the maze** — the route, the career, the struggle that builds
- **the lob / the finish** — contribution and credit, always paired
- **the pass** — every act of elevation; our core verb
- **the gallery for the game** — our positioning phrase
- **drop** — a collection release (Drop 01, Drop 02)

⚠️ Ration the metaphors. One maze or pass reference per piece of copy. Two reads
as a system; three reads as a gimmick.

### Words we never use
- ~~grind, hustle, no days off~~ — borrowed hustle-culture, not us
- ~~ball is life~~ — unless a contributor says it, quoted
- ~~exclusive, luxury, premium~~ as adjectives in customer-facing copy (we *are* it; saying it cheapens it. "Limited" and "numbered" are facts — those are fine)
- ~~iconic, legendary, GOAT~~ — inflation kills meaning
- ~~synergy, leverage, elevate your space~~ — corporate filler
- ~~journey~~ (the word) — we show routes, we don't narrate "journeys"
- Hype-stack punctuation: no "!!", no caps-lock words, max one exclamation point per page, ideally zero

### Grammar of the brand
- **Person:** third person for Damion · "we" for the org · "I" only in founder's letters
- **Case:** sentence case everywhere; lowercase acceptable on social; ALL CAPS only in the wordmark
- **Emoji:** none in editorial, shop, or sponsor surfaces. On social: at most one, and only when it does a job. The orange 🏀 never replaces the word basketball
- **Numbers:** numerals over words ("12 pieces," not "twelve pieces") in shop/deck copy; words allowed in editorial prose
- **Names:** people get full names on first mention, always. Photographers and artists get credited on every surface their work appears — captions, posts, product pages

## Before / after (calibration pairs)

**Shop product description**
- ❌ "This stunning, one-of-a-kind masterpiece celebrates the beautiful game with vibrant colors that will elevate any space!"
- ✅ "Alley Runner. One player, one lane, one shot. Edition of 50, numbered and signed. 18 × 27 archival print."

**Instagram launch post**
- ❌ "🚨🔥 IT'S HERE!! Our EXCLUSIVE first-ever drop is LIVE — don't miss out, these won't last! 🏀💯"
- ✅ "drop 01 is live. twelve pieces, fifty of each, numbered by hand. the maze is open."

**Sponsor deck line**
- ❌ "We offer best-in-class brand activation opportunities across our diversified content ecosystem."
- ✅ "Your brand inside the story, not next to it: co-created art series, a page in the print edition, a wall at the show."

**Athlete profile opener (magazine)**
- ❌ "From humble beginnings, her inspiring journey shows what hard work can achieve."
- ✅ "She was cut twice from the same team. The second time, she asked the coach for the scouting report on herself."

**About the founder**
- ❌ "A visionary entrepreneur and former elite athlete passionate about disrupting sports media."
- ✅ "He ran the maze — Pickering to Phoenix to Division I to a Nebraska juco and back. Then he passed the ball to his next self."

## The test

Before anything ships, read it aloud and ask three questions:

1. **Could a hype account have written this?** If yes, strip the heat until it's calm.
2. **Could a bank have written this?** If yes, add the court back in.
3. **Would Damion say this out loud to a teammate?** If no, it's not the voice.

The target is the narrow lane between those: *calm enough for the gallery, warm
enough for the court.*
